# Waste

Must every son
    suffer the sins of his father,
without healing
    quenching his thirst?

Our ancestors groan,
        I am sure,
    within the earth,
begging us to turn
    from their crooked lives.

So mountains
upon mountains
    lie before us:
        of the dying,
            of dead dirt.

    Though we rose from good soil,
        following temptation we return,
            (like waste)
        to litter the earth.
