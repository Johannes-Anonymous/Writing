# When I Was Young (And You Were Younger)

When I was young,
and you were younger,

you came back from Africa,
    and found me living
across from the gas station
    where I used to work.

Your smile was like an open flame
    that drew out my heart from its night,
and you became a reason for living,
    greater than just obedience to a paycheck.

So I gave up my old records
    at the thrift store;

I bought new shoes,
    without holes;

 and I cut my hair,
    for the first time
    in a long time.

And though it had been years
    without sight or sound of each other,
you were immediately familiar to me,
    even more so than before:

For, in the black caverns of my heart,
    unlit and unprobed by words,
your image had dwelt in silence,

    and I had dreamt of you often.
