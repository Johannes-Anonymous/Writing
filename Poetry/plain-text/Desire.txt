# Desire

Desire was a sharp blade,
    suddenly unsheathed by your face,
and I let my passions escape,
    unchecked and untamed.

While our town's steeple, ringing for us,
    I soon obtained,
you were already blossoming,
    a fresh garden in the rain.

Oh, our good wine and roses,
    I so casually tossed to the flames —

    Virtue was thrown down,
    Honor was ground down,
    True love was struck down;

A single evening pierced our hearts
    and drew blood from these streets;
your maidenhood was blotted out
    before your name ever changed.
