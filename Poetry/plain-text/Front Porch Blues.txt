# Front Porch Blues

Summer days fade like my memories,

    unraveling,

and growing thin like the moon.


Colored leaves quiver in the aimless breeze,

    beautiful momentarily;

Would you come back soon?



(the image that goes along with the poem)

From the back: a man (old?) in a rocking chair on a wooden front porch,
with an empty rocking or regular sitting chair beside him,
and a small wooden table in between.
Nothing is visible that didn't exist in the 19th century,
but nothing should give the impression that it is necessarily that old
(it could be in either the 19th or 21st century — it should have a “timeless” American farm feel).

On the table are two tall glasses.
The one closer to him is filled, and the other one is empty.
There is a bowl of lemon slices on the table, but no lemons in either glass.
It is a sunny day. The sun is not visible in the frame.
Much of the porch and man (probably all of both) are in the shade (the sun may be coming from overhead or behind the house.)
Maybe: the porch is overlooking rolling farm hills, with scattered groves of trees.
Undetermined: whether or not there is a pitcher on the table, or perhaps on the porch floor.
