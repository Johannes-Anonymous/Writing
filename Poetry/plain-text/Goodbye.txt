# Goodbye

We made it into town after sunset.

In the morning,
    at the outskirts,
we saw clumps of dead animals.

Something had been eating them.

The highways have been empty for a week,
and the radio is just static.

But something is following us.

Every off-ramp, every street is unfamiliar,
    and our stomachs growl.
Our bones tell us:
    we are being watched.

Yestermonth, we were home.
Today, we fidget, pace,
    under a now-rare tree.
Tomorrow,
    I hope,
we forever sleep…

Goodbye.
