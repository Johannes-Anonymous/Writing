# Anchors

Sails whip in tempest wind,
    salt soaks the quarters within.

Home, beyond sight,
    cannot match the siren's call.

Release, I must,
    anchors of trust
        against waves of dark fate:

    lest against Hell's black shore
        I break.



(the image that goes along with the poem)

- A large sailing vessel from the early 1800's, being tossed by massive waves in the ocean
- Nothing other than the ship, waves, and maybe some clouds are in the image
- The bow of the ship is greatly elevated from the rough seas
- The sky: left blank, or with storm clouds (no sun, stars, moon, or open sky)
- Perspective: 3/4 view of the ship, from the front
- The waves are massive, and coming from the image's left
- The style: like a wood cut or wood engraving or wood burned image (all natural wood colors)
- The text: the first two stanzas (“Sails whip…” and “Home, beyond sight…”) are at the top of the image, above the boat. The last stanzas (“Release, I must…”, etc.) are below the boat.
